HOW TO RECOGNIZE A GOOD BARBER
(Based on Gentlemen Barber Shop's own published guidance)

1. Their own appearance speaks first.
If the barber wears a beard, it should be perfectly maintained — thick, well-groomed, and precise. A barber's own appearance is his best calling card and strongest evidence of his daily commitment to the craft.

2. They understand your hair, not just your request.
A good barber listens carefully, asks questions about your lifestyle and how much time you invest in your hair at home, and adapts the cut accordingly. They don't just replicate what you describe — they translate it into what will actually work for you.

3. They know how to outline the beard line.
Defining the beard contour with precision is one of the most technically demanding skills in barbering. Only an experienced professional can handle this flawlessly. It's a reliable test of a barber's level.

4. They stay current on trends.
A skilled barber knows current styles — long beards, anchor beards, modern fades, textured cuts — and can advise you on what's trending and what suits your face shape.

5. They use and recommend the right products.
Professional-grade cosmetics for beard, facial skin, and hair care are a non-negotiable part of good barber practice. A great barber will also teach you how to use these products at home to maintain your look between visits.

BARBER vs. HAIRDRESSER — WHAT'S THE DIFFERENCE?

At a traditional hairdresser:
- Beard trimming typically costs 30–50 PLN
- Service is more standardized
- Focus is primarily on hair

At a barber shop:
- Beard trimming from 60–100 PLN
- Personalized consultation and advice is standard
- Full range of men's grooming: hair, beard, shaving, scalp, wax, Face SPA
- Results are tailored to the individual — both the cut and the ongoing care advice

The higher price at a barber includes a more comprehensive, personalized experience and professional guidance you won't get at a standard hair salon.

PREPARING FOR YOUR VISIT

First visit tips:
- Bring a reference photo if you have a specific style in mind
- Know roughly how much time you invest in styling at home (this helps the barber recommend the right cut)
- Don't wash your beard before a beard service — the barber will prep it during the service
- Arrive a few minutes early if it's your first time, to allow for a proper consultation

Maintenance between visits:
- Haircuts: recommended every 3–5 weeks for fades and tight styles; every 6–8 weeks for longer cuts
- Beard: trim or shape every 2–4 weeks depending on growth speed
- Ask your barber which products they recommend for your specific hair and beard type
